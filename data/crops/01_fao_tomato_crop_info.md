## Overview
Tomato is a fast-growing crop with a typical growing period of about 90 to 150 days, depending on climate and variety.

## Climate Requirements
- Optimal mean daily temperature: 18 to 25°C
- Sensitive to frost
- High humidity can increase pest, disease, and fruit rot risk

## Soil Requirements
- Can grow in many soil types
- Best in well-drained light loam
- Preferred soil pH: around 5 to 7
- Waterlogging increases disease risk

## Fertilizer Needs
High-producing tomato varieties may require approximately:
- Nitrogen: 100 to 150 kg/ha
- Phosphorus: 65 to 110 kg/ha
- Potassium: 160 to 240 kg/ha

## Water Requirements
- Approximate total water requirement after transplanting: 400 to 600 mm
- Actual need depends on climate, soil, and growth stage

## Critical Water-Sensitive Stages
Tomato is especially sensitive to water deficit:
- during and after transplanting
- during flowering
- during yield formation

## Irrigation Guidance
- Light, frequent irrigation is generally preferred
- Heavy irregular irrigation should be avoided
- Drip irrigation is suitable because it keeps soil moisture more stable and avoids wetting leaves

## Yield Notes
Yield depends on climate, management, irrigation, and variety.
