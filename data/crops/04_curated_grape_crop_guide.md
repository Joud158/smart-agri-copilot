## Overview
Grapes are a high-value crop widely suited to Mediterranean conditions when water stress, canopy management, and disease pressure are managed carefully.

## Climate Requirements
- Prefer warm, dry growing seasons
- Spring frost can damage early growth
- High humidity during sensitive stages raises mildew risk

## Soil Requirements
- Well-drained soils are preferred
- Moderate fertility is often better than excessive nitrogen
- Waterlogging raises disease and root stress risk
- A mildly acidic to neutral pH is generally suitable

## Water and Irrigation Notes
- Young vines need more regular support
- Mature vines often benefit from controlled irrigation rather than continuous excess watering
- Water stress close to fruit development should be monitored carefully

## Key Risk Notes
- Powdery mildew risk rises with poor airflow and humid conditions
- Canopy crowding can reduce air circulation
- Over-irrigation can reduce root aeration and increase disease pressure
