## Overview
Cucumber performs best under warm temperatures, regular irrigation, and stable soil moisture. Uneven watering can reduce fruit quality and increase stress.

## Climate Requirements
- Warm temperatures are preferred
- Chilling stress slows growth
- Very high humidity can increase disease pressure in protected cultivation

## Soil Requirements
- Fertile, well-drained soils are preferred
- Good organic matter helps moisture stability
- Waterlogging increases root disease risk

## Irrigation Guidance
- Frequent, moderate irrigation is usually better than severe wet-dry cycles
- Drip systems are useful because they stabilize root-zone moisture
- Flowering and fruit set are water-sensitive stages

## Key Risk Notes
- Whiteflies, aphids, and mildew can become important in protected cultivation
- Poor airflow increases disease pressure
- Hygiene and scouting should be consistent
