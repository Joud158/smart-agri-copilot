## Whiteflies
### Typical clues
- small white insects flying when plants are disturbed
- sticky honeydew on leaves
- leaf yellowing and general weakening

### Management direction
- improve monitoring with sticky traps
- remove heavily infested material where appropriate
- improve hygiene and exclusion in protected cultivation

## Aphids
### Typical clues
- clusters of soft-bodied insects on tender growth
- curled leaves
- sticky residue and reduced vigor

### Management direction
- scout young growth
- reduce weed hosts
- use IPM-first control logic

## Early Blight
### Typical clues
- brown lesions with concentric ring patterns
- lower leaves often affected first
- progression under plant stress and humid conditions

### Management direction
- remove infected leaves where practical
- avoid prolonged leaf wetness
- improve airflow and sanitation

## Powdery Mildew
### Typical clues
- white powdery patches on leaves and stems
- often worse with poor airflow

### Management direction
- reduce canopy humidity
- improve ventilation and spacing
- use crop-appropriate treatment guidance

## Yellow Spotting Caveat
Yellow spotting alone is not enough for confident diagnosis.
Possible causes include:
- nutrient imbalance
- mite pressure
- early disease development
- irrigation stress
- water-quality problems

The system should ask for crop, growth stage, location, and whether spotting is uniform or patchy.
