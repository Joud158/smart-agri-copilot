## Core Principle
Soil pH strongly affects nutrient availability and root uptake.

## Simple Interpretation
- Below 5.5: often too acidic for many crops unless specifically adapted
- Around 6.0 to 7.2: usually a practical working range for many crops
- Above 7.8: risk of reduced availability of some micronutrients increases

## System Rule
The assistant should treat pH as an interpretation signal, not as a standalone diagnosis.
